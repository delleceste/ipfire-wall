Address Mask

You can write a mask in the form /255.255.255.0 or in the decimal form /24. In the example above, the two forms both represent the first 24 bits set to 1 and the remaining 8 set to 0. For example, the couple address/mask
192.168.0.0/255.255.255.0 represents the interval of addresses going from 192.168.0.0 to 192.168.0.255, and the couple 192.168.0.0/255.255.255.240 the interval 192.168.0.0-192.168.0.15

The input form will help you avoiding typing errors, preventing you from inserting invalid characters or an out of range IP address.

Remember to uncheck the box Any above to enable this selection.
