The pre routing block

The packets coming from anywhere hit first of all the pre routing stage. There the routing decisions are taken, and each packet is inspected, particularly its destination IP address field. On the basis of its destination, the packet is delivered locally, to an application runnin on the machine (input block) or forwarded to another computer in the network, through the forward block.

Since the most important aspect taken into account here is the destination of a packet, here you are provided some tools to change the destination of the packet, both in terms of IP address and in terms or service (destination port).
This means:

  • adding a destination NAT rule.

Notes

  • Here one cannot filter a packet, i.e. cannot decide to drop or accept it.
  • The actions taken here are privilege of the administrator only.
Filtering can take place only in the input, output and forward stages.
