Filter on the port

Click on the combo box and choose among the options:

  • not to filter the packet on the port (ANY). This means that the filter rule will not consider at all the port number;
  • filter on the basis of the port, be it source or destination: with this option it is enough that the port provided matches any between the source port or the destination port(SOURCE OR DESTINATION);
  • filter on the basis of the source port (SOURCE);
  • filter on the basis of the destination port (DESTINATION);
  • filter on the basis of both the destination port and source port (SOURCE AND DESTINATION). In this case, two line edits will appear on the right of this combo box to let you fill in the two ports you want to filter on.

Remember that you will have to provide a complete and correct port, which is an integer number from 0 to 65535. Partial matches are not allowed because the filtering is done on the numeric port, not on its string representation. This means that a filter defined as SOURCE, 60 will match a packet with the source port equal to 60, but will not match a packet with a source port set to 6000 or 601.
