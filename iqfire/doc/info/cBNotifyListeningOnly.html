Notify attempts to connect to active services only

Your host usually has a set of services active and listening on a determined service access point, i.e. a particular port number. Hackers are usually interested in addressing their attacks towards active services, such as a spyware and other malicious software that might be running on your machine and listening on a particular port number.

For TCP connections, iqfire-wall is able to recognize what services are actually listening on your machine and warn you only when there is an attempt to connect to an active one.

On a host directly connected to the Internet, this option should reduce the amount of warnings for packets without an associated filter rule to the essential ones: those directed to the active services in your box.
