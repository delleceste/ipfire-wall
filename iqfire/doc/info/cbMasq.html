Masquerade

Masquerading a connection means changing the source address of a packet to let it appear as coming from a source different from the real address. With this kind of source nat it is possible, for multiple hosts in a private local area network, to connect to the external Internet through a gateway.
The gateway must be configured to masquerade the connections passing through the network interface connected to the Internet. In this way, all the packets originating from the internal network, with private addresses, are masqueraded when passing through the masquerade firewall/gateway and can connect to the external world, since they appear as if they were sent from the gateway, not from a private addressed machine.

As to the internal implementation, a masqueraded connection requires an associated table to remember the firewall that an outgoing masqueraded packet must be demasqueraded when coming back from the external world.
