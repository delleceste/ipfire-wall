The post routing block

All the packets leaving the machine, both those locally generated in the output chain and those forwarded pass through the post routing stage. There they will have all the necessary information to leave the host and reach another computer on the network.

In this phase, the packet can be modified to make it appear as leaving from another host, that is, source natting it.

Notes

  • Here one cannot filter a packet, i.e. cannot decide to drop or accept it.
  • The actions taken here are privilege of the administrator only.
Filtering can take place only in the input, output and forward stages.
