Filter on the IP address

Click on the combo box and choose among the options:

  • not to filter the packet on the IP address (ANY). This means that the filter rule will not consider at all the internet address;
  • filter on the basis of the internet address, be it source or destination: with this option it is enough that the address provided matches any between the source address or the destination address(SOURCE OR DESTINATION);
  • filter on the basis of the source network address (SOURCE);
  • filter on the basis of the destination address (DESTINATION);
  • filter on the basis of both the destination address and source address (SOURCE AND DESTINATION). In this case, two boxes will appear on the right of this combo box to let you fill in the two addresses you want to filter on.

Remember that you will have to provide a complete and correct IP address, in the form x.y.w.z, where x, y, w and z are integers between 0 and 255 (IPv4 address syntax). Partial matches (e.g. 192.168.0 or 192.168.1.) are not allowed because the filtering is done on the numeric address, not on its string representation.
